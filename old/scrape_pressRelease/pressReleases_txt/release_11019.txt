WASHINGTON- The Justice Department today announced an agreement with Lancaster County, Pa., to improve access to civic life for persons with disabilities. The agreement was reached under Project Civic Access (PCA), the department's wide-ranging initiative to ensure that cities, towns and counties throughout the country comply with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). The time for all local governments throughout the country to commit to achieving full ADA compliance is long overdue as we celebrate the 20th anniversary of this landmark civil rights law in July.
