A Montgomery, Alabama, resident pleaded guilty today to one count of a multi-object conspiracy to commit identity theft and wire fraud and one count of aggravated identity theft, announced Principal Deputy Assistant Attorney General Caroline D. Ciraolo of the Justice Departments Tax Division and U.S. Attorney George L. Beck Jr. of the Middle District of Alabama.NoneA date for Wells sentencing hearing has not yet been set. She faces a maximum sentence of five years in prison for conspiracy and a mandatory two-year prison sentence for aggravated identity theft, as well as a term of supervised release and monetary penalties.Principal Deputy Assistant Attorney General Ciraolo and U.S. Attorney Beck commended special agents of IRS-Criminal Investigation, who investigated the case and Trial Attorneys Jason H. Poole and Kathryn A. Kimball of the Tax Division and Assistant U.S. Attorney Jonathan S. Ross of the Middle District of Alabama, who are prosecuting this case.None
