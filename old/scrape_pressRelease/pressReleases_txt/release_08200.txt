  WASHINGTON  The owner and an employee of a Miami home health care agency were sentenced today to 108 months and 46 months in prison, respectively, for their participation in a $22 million Medicare fraud scheme, announced the Department of Justice, the FBI and the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS).  U.S. District Judge Patricia A. Seitz in Miami sentenced Marietha Morales, 38, to 108 months in prison and Eduardo Saborit-Dominguez, 48, to 46 months in prison. Both defendants were each sentenced to three years of supervised release. In addition, Morales was ordered to pay $14 million in restitution and Dominugez was ordered to pay $2 million in restitution, jointly and severally with each other.
