A dual Jamaican and U.S. citizen charged in connection with the operation of a Jamaican-based fraudulent lottery scheme was sentenced in Charlotte, North Carolina, the Department of Justice announced today. Felecia Roxanne Lindo, 33, was sentenced to serve 24 months in prison and three years supervised release by U.S. District Court Judge Robert J. Conrad Jr. in Charlotte. Lindo was also ordered to pay $292,900 in restitution.  Lindo pleaded guilty on Sept. 28, 2016, to one count of conspiracy to commit wire fraud, in the Western District of North Carolina. As part of her guilty plea, Lindo acknowledged that from in or about 2011 through at least in or about September 2012, she was a member of a lottery fraud conspiracy that targeted victims in the United States.
